Research

My research work is at the boundary between mathematics and application - for biological modelling in particular. I have been developing models, methods and algorithms for neurodegenerative diseases and oncological applications.
Below is a list of problems I have dealt with - the most recent ones first.

Model and inference of protein dynamics in neurodegenerative diseases

Nowadays my major interest is in the combined application of dynamical systems and disease progression techniques for modelling neurodegenerative diseases development and progression in terms of pathological protein propagation in the brain.
Here the long--term group--wise amyloid protein progression and propagation parameteres in Alzheimer's Disease on three brain regions of interest (superior frontal, lingual and thalamus) as estimated by my GPPM--ACP model. (IPMI paper).

The model con also be personalised to estimate individual disease progression, by integrating the estimated protein dynamics over time. Here an example for a subject-specific predicted amyloid accoumulation over time. Code for visualisation of 3D brains is brain painter.

Topological progression profiles of neurodegenerative diseases

We have introduced the notion of a topological profile — a characteristic combination of connectomics-based descriptors that best describes the propagation of pathology in a particular disease.
Here the long--term neurodegeneration patterns as estimated by my Topological Profiles Model (paper and github repository) from short--term T1-MRI data of Alzheimer's disease data (left) compared with the actual AD progression (right). Code for visualisation of 3D brains is brain painter.

Compartmental model for glucose metabolism in cancerous tissues

My PhD studies have been mainly devoted to the study of physiological and pathological behaviour of glucose metabolism in tissue, using micro-PET data. I have been modelling the hepatic and the renal metabolism, under normal or cancerous conditions. I have studied the impact of anticancer drugs by measuring the effects on the parameters estimated by my models.
Here the model for glucose metabolism in the liver and results on mice when treated with metformin, as opposed to control or fasted mice. We observed a significant decrease of the coefficients describing glucose absorption.
